scrvUSD is short for savings-crvUSD, and it functions like a bank savings account for crvUSD.  It is similar to other staked versions of USD stablecoins like sDOLA, sUSDS, sUSDe, etc.  Users can stake their crvUSD to receive scrvUSD, or swap for it directly.  Yield accrues automatically to scrvUSD holders, increasing the value of their tokens over time.

## How scrvUSD works

The way scrvUSD works is very simple, it's called a savings vault because it works like a bank savings account:

1. Deposit crvUSD to the vault and receive scrvUSD in return
2. Hold your scrvUSD
3. Over time your scrvUSD will be worth more crvUSD
4. Withdraw crvUSD from the scrvUSD vault by sending back scrvUSD tokens, you'll receive your initial deposit and any earned interest

### How much will I earn?

The interest rate (yield) is variable and changes every few days, the current and historical rates are all shown on the [scrvUSD UI](https://www.curve.finance/crvusd/ethereum/scrvUSD/).  To understand what the different numbers mean, see this section [FAQ: What do the different APR/APY numbers on the UI mean?](scrvusd.mdx#what-do-the-different-aprapy-numbers-on-the-ui-mean)

The interest rate scrvUSD holders earn is roughly equal to the previous week's crvUSD average borrow rate for all the [crvUSD minting markets](https://www.curve.finance/crvusd/ethereum/markets/), unless there's more than 50% of crvUSD staked, then yield will be slightly diluted, see the table here to see how much crvUSD is currently staked: [Current scrvUSD Stats](../curve-tokens/scrvusd.mdx#current-scrvusd-stats).

### Where does the yield come from?

When users borrow (mint) crvUSD, they pay a borrowing interest rate while their loan remains open.  scrvUSD receives up to 50% of these fees, and the Curve DAO receives the rest. This means the underlying crvUSD is not loaned out, or deployed to swap pools, it remains safely within the vault, minimizing risk.

To deposit into the savings vault and start earning interest, first you need crvUSD on Ethereum mainnet.  If you don't have any crvUSD read our guide here on how to swap for crvUSD on Curve's DEX: [How to Swap on Curve's DEX](../dex/guides/how-to-swap).

### Withdrawing from the Savings Vault

You can withdraw from the Vault at any time, there is no delay or lock at all.  Whenever you want to withdraw simply go to the [scrvUSD vault](https://www.curve.finance/crvusd/ethereum/scrvUSD/) and click on the withdraw tab on the left.  It will show you how many scrvUSD tokens you have, you can choose an amount to withdraw, or click "MAX" to withdraw all:

## FAQ

### What do the different APR/APY numbers on the UI mean?

On the [scrvUSD UI](https://www.curve.finance/crvusd/ethereum/scrvUSD/) you'll see a screen like the following:

<figure style={{ textAlign: 'center' }}>
  <ThemedImage
    alt="scrvUSD statistics"
    sources={{
      light: require('@site/static/img/ui/savings/statistics-light.png').default,
      dark: require('@site/static/img/ui/savings/statistics-dark.png').default,
    }}
    style={{
      maxWidth: '933px',
      width: '100%'
    }}
  />
  <figcaption></figcaption>
</figure>

Above you can see there are a few different numbers shown, here's what they mean:

- **Current APY**: Current interest rate with compounding included
- **APR**: Interest rate at that time without compounding
- **7-day MA APR**: Average interest rate for the 7 days before that time, without compounding
- **Average APR**: Average interest rate without compounding for your chosen time window, e.g. above in the bottom right corner 1M (1 month) is chosen, so this is the average interest rate for the last month
