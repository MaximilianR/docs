DeFi – or decentralized finance – is a movement to build a financial system that’s open, transparent, and controlled by its users, not big institutions. It’s like rebuilding banks, exchanges, and lending platforms from scratch — but this time, anyone can participate, no permission needed.

Curve Finance is one of the core building blocks of that new system.

At its heart, Curve is a place to swap stablecoins efficiently, earn rewards on your crypto, and take out loans using your own assets as collateral – all without giving up control of your funds. Whether you’re trading one dollar-pegged coin for another, earning yield by providing liquidity, or borrowing Curve’s decentralized stablecoin (crvUSD), Curve offers powerful tools for making your money work harder in the world of DeFi.

But what really sets Curve apart is the way it’s governed: not by a company or CEO, but by a global community of token holders. If you participate, you can help shape how Curve evolves – from which tokens are rewarded, to how fees are distributed.

---

## **What can you do on Curve?**

Curve lets you do a few powerful things, all from your own crypto wallet:

- **Swap stablecoins and other tokens efficiently** — Curve is designed to give you the best prices when trading similar assets like USDC, DAI, or crvUSD, with minimal fees and slippage.
- **Earn passive income by providing liquidity** — You can deposit tokens into Curve pools and earn trading fees, CRV rewards, and even additional yield from partner protocols.
- **Vote and participate in governance** — By locking CRV tokens into veCRV, you get to vote on how the protocol works, where rewards go, and even earn a share of the platform’s fees.
- **Borrow Curve’s decentralized stablecoin, crvUSD** — You can deposit collateral like ETH and mint crvUSD to use elsewhere, while keeping exposure to your crypto.

Boost your rewards — If you’re a long-term Curve user, locking CRV gives you the ability to boost your yields and even earn extra rewards through community “bribes.”

In short, Curve gives you tools to trade, earn, borrow, and participate — all without relying on a bank or middleman.

---

## **Why does Curve matter in DeFi?**

Curve is one of the most important pieces of the DeFi puzzle.

Most of DeFi runs on stablecoins — tokens like USDC, DAI, and crvUSD that are designed to stay close to $1. But for stablecoins to be useful, they need to have deep liquidity. That’s where Curve comes in. Its unique design makes it the most efficient place to trade similar-value assets, powering stablecoin swaps behind the scenes for dozens of apps and protocols.

Curve also introduced one of the most influential token models in DeFi: veCRV, where users lock tokens for long-term governance power and boosted rewards. This created the so-called “Curve Wars”, as other protocols started competing for influence in Curve’s reward system — and it changed how DeFi thinks about incentives.

Today, Curve is much more than just a place to swap stablecoins. It’s a decentralized exchange, a stablecoin issuer, a yield engine, and a community-governed protocol — all rolled into one. That’s why Curve matters.
